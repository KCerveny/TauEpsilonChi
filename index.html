Our Vision

TEX was founded to tackle the big problems that we see with greek life in our universities. Our goal is to see fraternities foster meaningful friendships and act as forces for good in their communities. Unfortunately, time and time again we have seen fraternities around the country struggle with the same problems.

Protecting New Members

The word "pledge" is most often assiciated with hazing and its many forms. Here at TEX, we view hazing as a conflict to our core values, especially in promoting meaningful friendship and enhancing the academic success of our members. For these reasons, TEX does not have pledges, but instead candidates.

  • No-Tolerance Policy for Hazing
  • Candidate Bill of Rights

Accessibility to all People

Fraternity dues often run in the thousands of dollars per semester for many campuses around the US. This makes them automatically inaccessable to many students who do not come from upper-class families, which prevents many students from participating, and serves to make fraternities less diverse and inclusive.

At TEX, we are committed to making greek life inclusive to all kinds of people. We pride ourselves on having some of the lowest dues on campus, and have eliminated most of the costs associated with becoming a new member. In addition, we provide all of our members options for dynamic payment plans and scholarships to keep their dues managable.

Respect for Women

Unfortunately, it seems that there is a strong truth to the advice "don't drink the punch" at frat parties. Most students can tell you about a female friend who was subject to a preadatory environment at a fraternity event.

In TEX, we have made strong efforts to have our female friends feel comfortable and included at all of our events. In our public events, you will find posted signs around all of our venues with guidance on what to do if someone is making you uncomfortable, along with designated members vigilantly monitoring our events. In addition, we are constantly working with leaders in sororities and spirit groups to create policies protecting our guests and creating a comfortable environment.

Membership

Instead of a conventional pledgeship, tentative TEX members go through a process called Candidacy, where active members evaluate their fit with the Tau Epsilon Chi values and culture.

What is Candidacy?

Candidacy is a dynamic process of evaluation of potential members to learn about the strengths and weaknesses of the candidates on an individual level, and to allow the candidates to interact with each other to get to know the active members of TEX. Candidacy will be short; it will only be 14 days long. This means that you should have sufficient time to manage the activities during candidacy without an undue burden on your other responsibilities.

What happens during Candidacy?

During the Candidacy process, each Candidate will be asked to participate in a number of team building activities and individual projects which will involve using effective planning, time management, and communication skills. Each of these activities should not be excessively monotonous, but should allow each candidate to experience what working under each branch of the organization is like and what factors go into the normal day to day operations of the fraternity.

What are the projects during Candidacy?

Each Candidate will participate in a Leadership Development Project (LDP), a Personal Project (PP), and two seminars during the course of Candidacy. The LDP is a team project with teammates you have been assigned in the chart below. The objective of the LDP is to work together to comprehensively plan an event for the fraternity. The kind of event and time will be assigned to you, but the remaining details you will be responsible for. The Personal Project is an individual task that will be randomly assigned to you, but the effort and thought involved will be obvious from the output of the project. The two seminars will be released in a schedule on Slack, our communication interface. The subject of the seminars will pertain to the problems common in greek organizations or leadership development. Each team will be assigned a mentor for accomplishing their LDP, but individuals can reach out to their mentor or Wayde Adler at any time during Candidacy for assistance of any kind.

How much are the dues for TEX?

The dues for TEX are priced per semester. New members pay $400 for their first semester, and then $350 per semester after that. This covers almost all events associated with TEX, but on occasion there are additional events that are subject to extra cost.

What is the time commitment for TEX?

As an active member, you can come to as many or as few events throughout a semester as you choose to. On a normal week, TEX will hold one chapter meeting at the house on Monday evenings, as well as 1-2 additional official events. Active members also often hang out unofficially, planning meetups or game nights in the #hangouts channel of the TEX Slack.
